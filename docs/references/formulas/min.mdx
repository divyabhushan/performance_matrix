The `MIN` function returns the smallest number in a set of values. Node references also accepted as arguments. 

## Syntax
```javascript
MIN (value1,[value2]....)
```
## Arguments
```value1,value2 …`` – The nodes or numbers of which you want the smallest of. 

## Example
Consider a node which has the demand for a particular product for 12 months. 

| Month  | Jan | Feb | Mar | Apr | May | Jun | Jul | Aug | Sep | Oct | Nov | Dec |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Demand | 180 | 200 | 238 | 174 | 286 | 319 | 265 | 136 | 220 | 260 | 300 | 315 |

To calculate the minimum demand, the formula should be 
```javascript
MIN (Demand.ALL_PERIODS)
``
The minimum demand is calculated to be 136.
